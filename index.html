04

Case Study

International Business Email Fraud

$415,017.58 Supplier Payment

Shivganga Drillers, an industrial company based in Indore, India, was preparing a payment of $415,017.58 to Innovex International Inc, a vendor in Houston.

Cybercriminals spoofed the vendor’s email, changed the banking details, and redirected the payment to a fraudulent JP Morgan Bank account in the United States.

The fraud was discovered after another spoofed email claimed the payment had been rejected and requested a new transfer. The company verified directly with the vendor and alerted the cyber cell.

The funds were eventually recovered after emergency coordination between Indian cyber authorities, IC3, JP Morgan Bank and Bank of India.

What Existing Systems Saw

  • Legitimate Business Communication
  • Known Supplier Relationship
  • Approved Internal Process
  • Authorized Payment Request
  • No Technical Access Violation
